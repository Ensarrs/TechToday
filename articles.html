New Research Waves Red Flag Over Gaming Scams Aimed at Kids

October 8, 2024 5:00 AM PT by John P. Mello Jr.

A report from the Mobile Premier League (MPL) highlights the growing threat of cybercriminals targeting young gamers through online scams.
These scams exploit children's innocence and lack of experience, putting them at risk of identity theft, unauthorized access to bank accounts, and online harassment.
Young gamers are especially vulnerable because they often have difficulty distinguishing between real-life and fictional scenarios, making it easier for scammers to manipulate them. Cybercriminals often gain trust by offering in-game gifts or promises of friendship, eventually tricking children into sharing personal information, clicking on phishing links, or downloading malware disguised as game modifications.
Popular games like Roblox, Minecraft, Fortnite, and Apex Legends are frequent targets, with phishing attempts and malicious content specifically created for these platforms. For example, in 2022, over 878,000 phishing pages were made for these games, with Roblox alone accounting for the majority. To protect their children, parents are encouraged to enable parental controls, restrict in-app purchases, and educate children about online safety.
Regular monitoring of gaming activities and fostering open communication can help identify potential threats early. Parents should also set clear boundaries, limit screen time, and ensure that children play age-appropriate games on safe platforms.
These preventive measures are crucial for shielding children from the rising threat of online gaming scams.
